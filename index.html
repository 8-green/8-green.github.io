Cross The Bridge

Bridges are powerful structures that seem like triangles on the outside, but they dive deeper than you can imagine. Bridges represent connecting 2 ideas, transitioning from one phase to another, or journeying through a difficult obstacle. In literature, crossing a bridge symbolizes a change in mindset, the act of leaving the past behind. Bridges can also be used in the opposite sense, when bridging toward your past, finding out about yourself. Bridges can be used to find out about your family heritage and your ancestors. A broken bridge symbolizes breaking a relationship, not being able to forget the past, or an impossible barrier to cross. Bridging from 7th grade to 8th grade is a big deal, and in this sense the word bridge is used for transitioning from one phase to the other, although one can argue this can also be overcoming 7th grade. To bridge from 7th to 8th grade, you need many skills such as organization, task initiation, and more. As an 8th grader going to 9th grade, I learned to be on top of things and learned to organize my stuff, as I hope you guys will learn on your bridge here. In conclusion, bridges are symbols that represent many different ideas, ranging from crossing an unstoppable barrier to breaking ties with the past, and bridging the gap between 7th and 8th grade will be a hard but fun experience.

Executive Functioning Skills

To have a successful year in 8th grade, you need to have a multitude of executive functioning skills:

Goal Directed Persistence

Goal directed persistence is the ability to set a goal and work towards it, without getting distracted by distractions or other tasks. This is important because you need to focus to get work done on time.

Prioritization

Figure out which one of your priorities is most important, and organize your time correctly by deciding what can wait and what can’t. This is important in 8th grade because sometimes you will have a lot of projects to work on and you have to decide the order you should work on them based on their due date.

Task Initiation

Task initiation is the skill of starting a task early, and not waiting until the last minute and procrastinating. This is important because sometimes you aren’t able to judge how much time something takes, leading you to not finish on time.

Organization

Organization is the skill of staying organized and keeping all your materials neat and orderly. This is important because it is necessary to bring all your materials to class for a good grade.

Response Inhibition

Response Inhibition is the skill of thinking ahead before you do something and thinking about the consequences of a certain action. This skill will stop you from acting rashly.

Interpersonal & Social Skills

An 8th grade student should show strong interpersonal and social skills because these skills help them work well with others, make friends, and handle everyday school situations. Middle school is a time when friendships, group work, and peer pressure become more common.

Respectful Interaction

Good interpersonal skills include listening carefully, speaking politely, showing respect, and working well in groups. At this age, students are expected to communicate clearly and respectfully with classmates, teachers, and other adults, take turns in conversation, follow directions, and understand how their words and actions affect other people.

Social Intelligence

Good social skills also include empathy, teamwork, self-control, and solving conflicts in a calm way. Being able to read social cues, such as body language and tone of voice, helps them respond appropriately in different situations. An 8th grade student who has strong social skills is usually better able to cooperate, resolve disagreements, and build positive relationships, supporting academic success.
